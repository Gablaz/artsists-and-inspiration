Frida Kahlo

Frida Kahlo (July 6, 1907-July 13, 1954) was a mexican artist best known for her self-portraits due to the message that she conveyed and the bold and vibrant colors as well. Kahlo was suffering from polio and nearly died in a bus accident where she suffered multiple fractures. While she was gradually recovering, she spent most of her time in bed painting self-portraits. Kahlo was painting her life experiences which had such strong messages about what she dealt with emotionally and physically. One of her well known works is the “Broken Column” because it represented how she fractured her spine due to the bus accident.
